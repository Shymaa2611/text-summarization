in the first sense, ‘inference’ means a process of thought or reasoning by which the mind passes from facts or statements presented, to some opinion or expectation. the data may be very vague and slight, prompting no more than a guess or surmise. in the second sense, ‘inference’ means not this process of guessing or opining, but the result of it; the surmise, opinion, or belief when formed.
