the doctrine of Terms, words, or other signs used as subjects or predicates. the doctrine of Propositions, analysed into terms related, and the doctrine of the Syllogism in which propositions appear as the grounds of a conclusion. the terms employed are either letters of the alphabet, or the words of common language, or the technicalities of science. it has been urged that we cannot think or reason at all without words, or some substitute for them, such as the signs of algebra.
